Top three places to visit in Karimnagar

Lower Manair Dam

The Lower Manair Dam (LMD) is a significant engineering structure on the Manair River near Karimnagar, Telangana, serving as a crucial water source for agriculture and drinking water in the region. Completed in 1985, the dam is a major tourist destination, known for its scenic beauty, especially when the floodgates are open during the monsoon.

Nagunur Fort

Nagunur Fort is a historical site in Telangana, near Karimnagar, known for its ruins of temples dating back to the Kakatiya and Kalyani Chalukya eras, particularly between the 12th and 13th centuries. It was a significant political and religious center, possibly once housing around 400 temples, with the central Shiva temple being a notable structure featuring three shrines and intricate carvings.

Elgandal Fort

Elgandal Fort, located near Karimnagar on the Manair River, is a historic hill fort with roots in the Kakatiya dynasty and subsequent rule by the Qutb Shahi, Mughal, and Nizam periods. Visitors can explore its well-preserved fortifications, 300 stone steps, and sites like the Brundavan Pond, mosques, and tombs of revered Muslim saints, offering a glimpse into the region's layered past. The fort provides panoramic views of the countryside and the Lower Manair Dam.
